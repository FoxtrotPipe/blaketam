Project 1: If toilet system were more user-friendly

Brainstorming

We started by picking out the small incoveniences in our daily campus life and ask ourselves which of these incoveniences annoy us the most. Eventually, each of us came up with a few ideas and we grouped them into this mindmap.

At last, we decided to tackle the "toilet issue" in our campus.

Problem scope

Some toilets in the campus always have its cubicles preoccupied, while there are still some vacancies in other toilets, especially in rush hour. We find out that a lot of UST students find it inconvenient when you go into toilet and find out all cubicles are occupied. The toilet facilities in campus are therefore not fully utilised.

We discover the situation where students are entering and leaving the washroom within a short period. This happens especially around 12:00, 13:30, 15:00, which are breaks between lectures. Some lesser-known washrooms are not utilized as frequently as washrooms near hotspots, eg. Library, LT-A, LG1 canteen…

All of us are active users of UST’s washroom. We have all experienced the aforementioned toilet-finding problem. We all agree that different washrooms were not utilized equally, resulting in some crowding while the others having low usage.

Our final solution - a smart toilet system

We think that our campus is in urgent need for a smart toilet system. What do we mean by this? We will design an app that serves to show all available toilet cubicles directly on your mobile phone. This app may be embedded into the existing HKUST Student / Staff app as a plugin too. With this app, student & staff may now be able to know where is the closest washroom with empty slot.

On the technical level, The app will have modern, simplistic and intuitive UI so that it is easy to use. We will also design the app such that the toilet result's latency time is within seconds so that users get to see the result quickly.

My contribution & reflection

In my group, I helped to create part of the group presentation ppt by writing ppt text and searching ppt images. I was also responsible for introducing the "Smart Toilet System App" part in our group presentation.

After this project, I've learnt the importance of thinking from the users' point of view while developing apps. It is always to easy to implement cool and new technologies into an app, but without any consideration and empathy for the app users, the app could become an inconvenience and annoyance regardless of what it could achieve. It is important for us to remember that when we are designing an app, we are trying to solve real-world problems and not create hicups for the users. If we pay extra attention to create a cohesive and smooth user experience for the app, then we will truly achieve this goal.

Project 2: Chatbot for human wellbeing

Brainstorming

Our group drafted several storyboards that illustrate scenarios where chatbots can promote user physical, academic and financial wellbeing. After speed dating with some HKUST students, we figured out that a lot of students find managing a healthy lifestyle (i.e. physical wellbeing) to be a challenge.

Finally, we decided to build a health assitant chatbot that focuses on users' physical wellbeing.

Problem scope

We started by trying to understand why a health assitant chatbot would be needed. We discover a lot of people are conscious of their health amid their busy lives. They often want to seek health advice and exercise schedule that is catered to their personal need, body type etc.

These health advices that are personal or specific are often not easy to find on the internet for ordinary users. Although these advices may exist on the internet, most users are often not knowledgeable or professioned enough that they can correctly pick our these advices according to the biological and medical status of their body. Our health bot seeks to address this issue.

Prototype - Health assitant chatbot

We designed a prototype chatbot that seeks to help users maintain a healthy lifestyle. Our bot achieves this by helping users to (1) maintain a healthy eating habit and (2) a stable exercise schedule. As for the functions of the bot, it can keep track of the nutrition intake of the users (e.g. how much sugar, sodium the user has consumed) and even suggest healthy eating choices to users according to their intake.

The bot can also keep track of users' physical activity level so that users know if they've done enough exercise for one week. Since our bot is designed to resemble a personal assitant, the bot will tend to encourage and motivate users whenever the users are detected to be unmotivated about doing sports (e.g. when the user tells the bot "I'm feeling lazy. I don't want to do exercise today").

The bot can also suggest sports for specific fitness training including sports of different types (e.g. weighlifting, stretching), focus muscle group (e.g. biceps, chest) and difficulty (e.g. beginner, intermediate).

My contribution & refleciton

In our group, I helped to program the exercise suggestion function of the prototype bot and add response variety to the bot. I was also responsible for the video demo part of our presentation.

After this project, I've learnt the importance of focusing on user need as a design motive. Since chatbot is an amusing technolog at first glance, obviously a lot of fancy ideas can be brainstormed through this cool technology. However, if we only make a chatbot without considering user need and usability, then the bot would be possibly of no use and perform even worse when compared to a traditional software. This is why while in this modern time we have so many amazing technologies, such as chatbot, at our hands, it is important to understand that we are utilizing this technology to address the need of users and keep this idea in mind during the design of softwares related to these technologies.

Project 3: XR for Lifelong Learning

Brainstorming

As the focus of this project is lifelong learning, we started by brainstorming what kinds of skills that people may want to learn throughout their lives. In the process, we realize "skill learning" may have different meanings to different groups of people, such as for children and teens, they learn skills mainly for personal growth. As for elderly, they learn skills to keep track of the current technologies. We drew storyboards to illustrate several scenarios where users can learn skills at any time and any place with XR:

As you can see, such a XR platform would have the potential to make skill-learning easy for many people. That's why we have decided to prototype and propose such a system in our project.

Problem Scope

Next, we delve into why some people would specifically want to use XR to learn skills. We realize that urban dwellers with busy lives sometimes just don't have enough time or aren't physically capable to enroll in any class (i.e. accessibility issue). They want to acquire new skills for a variety of purposes, which could include enhancing personal development, keeping up with technological trends etc. The major goal of this system is to provide everyone an efficient and interactive way to learn new life skills.

Prototype - 1 to 1 XR Masterclass

We utilized VRChat to prototype our proposed "1-to-1 XR Masterclass". We understand that different groups of people may desire to learn skills so it present a challenge for our platform to provide tutoring service for every skills. To resolve this issue, we have decided the XR Masterclass platform should adopt a private-tutoring and subscription-based model. People from all walks of life can become private tutors on the platform and hold courses on their professions for platform users. My Contribution & Reflection

Within our group, I was responsible for part of the presentation ppt and doing the introduction part of the presentation. After this project, I have learnt that implementing XR into part of our lives are often more complicated than we think. Although extended reality create a more interactive experience and open up a lot of possibility of modifying our "real world" (in a vision sense). However in practical implementation, it introduces a lot of HCI-related complications, such as users might experience motion sickness when attending long XR courses and elderly might not be familiar with controls of XR. That's why in the real world, it is always important to experience your technological invention from the users' perspective and emphasize with the users.
